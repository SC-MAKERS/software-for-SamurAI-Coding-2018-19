How to Use SamurAI Jockey 2018 Race Viewer

SamurAI Jockey Race Viewer is a tool to view race results of the SamurAI Jockey game on HTML browsers.

Display

The figure below shows a sample display image of the race viewer. On the top is the control panel with several buttons and sliders for playback control. The next row is the race state panel that shows some information on the state of the race. At the bottom, there are two images of the race course. One on the right hand side shows the whole course while the other on the left hand side shows only the part of interest of the course magnified.

Controlling the Viewer

Buttons and sliders as shown below are in the control panel at the top of the window.

Load Log Button

Clicking on this button shows a dialog to select a log file of a race of SamurAI Jockey game. Log files are generated by SamurAI Jockey Official system. An example log file samples/sample.racelog is included in the distribution of SamurAI Jockey system.

When a race log file is loaded, a course overview and a magnified view near the start position are displayed.

Start/Stop Button

When race playback is not running, the start/stop button has the text Start on it. Clicking it starts or resumes automatic playback of the race.

When a race playback is running, the button has the text Stop onit, and clicking it will pause the playback.

Playback will automatically stops when the race is finished.

Stepping Buttons

Buttons marked < and > are stepping buttons. Clicking them single-steps the game state backward and forward, respectively.

Rewind Button

Cliking the rewind button marked << restore the race state to its start.

Speed Slider

The slider in the control panel is for controlling the speed of the playback. Moving the slider to the left/right will slow down/speed up the playback. The current speed is shown in terms of steps per minute on the right.

Help Button

Cliking the help button shows this page.

Keyboard Shortcuts

The following keyboard shortcuts can also be used.

  • s: start/stop
  • r: rewind
  • f: step forward
  • b: step backward
  • +: speed up
  • -: slow down

    Game State Information

    The information panel below the control panel shows some information of the state of the game.

    Step Number

    Next to the right most clock icon, the current step number is displayed.

    Player Names and Positions

    Names and current positions of two players are displayed next. The text characters of red and blue are used for correspoinding players. When a player has finished the race, its goal time is displayed in place of its position.

    Race Course Display

    The race course, current positions of two players, their trajectories, and the specified moves at the step are displayed on the race course display.

    The whole race course is dispayed on the right while the part of interest of the course is displayed on the left magnified.

    Grid Points and Obstructs

    Yellow dots indicate grid points on the race course. Brown dots, brown line segments, and areas filled brown indicate obstructs.

    Start and Finish Lines

    The start and finish lines are shown as white lines.

    Player Icons

    Players' positions and their current directions are indicated with triangles of respective colors.

    Trajectories

    Trajectories of players, i.e., the paths players have taken, are shown as polygonal lines of their colors.

    Indicated Moves

    Moves of players instructed to take at the step (movement lines) are shown as gray line segments starting from the player icons. The line segments end at circles of the players' colors indicating the planned positions of the players.

    When a player cannot move as planned because of course outs or collisions with the opponent, the movement line is shown as a dashed line.
